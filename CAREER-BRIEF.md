## 2. The anchor title: "AI Product Engineer"

This is the single phrase every surface should push.

**Why this title (and not others):**
- "Software Engineer" — too generic, hides the AI work that's my edge.
- "Full-Stack Developer" — accurate but positions me as a generalist, not a specialist. I want to signal AI depth.
- "ML Engineer" / "Research Engineer" — overclaims pure-research identity. I ship products, not just papers.
- "Creative Engineer" / "Creative Developer" — **banned**. This is a design-adjacent phrase and misreads my work.
- **"AI Product Engineer"** — says I build *shipping products that have AI in them*, and I do it end-to-end. Matches how I actually work.

**Use this phrase verbatim** in the resume header, LinkedIn headline, and cover-letter opening.

## 3. What I'm NOT (phrases + framings to avoid)

- ❌ "Creative engineer", "creative developer", "creative coder"
- ❌ "Designer who codes" / "developer with design sense"
- ❌ "Co-Founder" or "CTO" — **never mention either title anywhere**, even though the role at EONVERSE is senior. Use **"Founding Engineer"** instead.
- ❌ "Full-stack generalist" — accurate but flattening.
- ❌ "Passionate about technology" / "rockstar developer" / "tech enthusiast" — filler adjectives.
- ❌ "Proficient in X, Y, Z" as a sentence opener — skills belong in the skills section, not prose.

## 4. Voice rules

- **Curiosity-led, builder-first.** The through-line is "curious enough to try, disciplined enough to ship."
- **Concrete over abstract.** Every bullet should name a system, a tool, or a metric. "Built an agentic pipeline" > "contributed to AI initiatives."
- **Architecture-forward for EONVERSE.** When describing work there, lead with the *technical shape* (multi-agent, shared state, custom consistency engine), not the product domain.
- **Research credibility is a differentiator.** Four IEEE-published papers as a new grad is unusual — weight it accordingly.
- **No filler adjectives.** Kill "passionate", "innovative", "dynamic", "robust", "cutting-edge", "leveraging".
- **Past tense, active voice.** "Shipped", "built", "designed", "reduced", "owned".

## 6. EONVERSE — the NDA boundary ⚠️

This is the most important rule. Read twice.

**Role title:** Founding Engineer. (Not Co-Founder. Not CTO.)

**What I CAN discuss publicly:**
- Architecture: multi-agent / agentic media pipelines
- Technical patterns: shared state layers, coordination between models
- Custom consistency engines (generic term — keeps outputs stable across steps)
- Infra, ops, deployment patterns
- Team scale, my ownership areas

**What I CANNOT discuss publicly (NDA):**
- The product idea itself
- Words/domain hints that would reveal it: **script, storyboard, film, movie, scene, character consistency** — all banned from resume/LinkedIn/public docs
- Specific use-case of the pipeline
- Clients, partners, investors

**Redaction test:** If a stranger could guess the product category from my bullet, redact more. Keep it at pure architecture.

**Safe template bullet:**
> "Founding Engineer at EONVERSE. Built an agentic media pipeline coordinating multiple models through a shared state layer, with a custom consistency engine maintaining output stability across steps. Product under NDA; architecture is not."

## 10. The audience I'm writing for

Not a recruiter with a keyword filter. **An engineering lead reading the resume after the recruiter passes it on.** That person wants to see:

- Shipping evidence (live URLs, GitHub, measurable outcomes)
- Technical depth (architecture, not just tools)
- That I can learn fast (curiosity + volume of things tried)
- That I won't need hand-holding (Founding-Engineer-level ownership)

Write every bullet to pass that reader, not the keyword filter.
